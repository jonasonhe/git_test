Rack of Lamb from Gordon Ramsay's paris Steakhouse (made from the lamb sauce that Gordon's been searching for)

Description

This is the signature rack of lamb recipe from Gordon Ramsay's Paris Steakhouse from Las Vegas

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup basil leaves, packed
  • 1/4 cup fresh flat leaf parsley, packed 8 ounces panko bread crumbs
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan, grated
  • 2 New Zealand rack of lamb, 14 to 16 ounces, 7 to 8 bone, frenched, fat cap removed
  • 2 tablespoons grapeseed oil Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 bunch thyme
  • 2 tablespoons butter, cubed 4 tablespoons Dijon mustard

  • Maldon or kosher salt to taste

Steps

  1. Make the basil crust by putting all the ingredients in a blender until bright green and evenly processed. Taste and add salt or more parmesan if needed
  2. Preheat oven to 375F and place lamb racks on a sheet
  3. Press all sides of the meat into the remaining seasoning on the sheet tray
  4. Add the lamb racks to the pan bone side up with the racks resting on the edge of the pans
  5. Add the thyme, garlic, and butter to the pan and baste for another miniute moving the toasted thyme and garlic to the back of the lamb racks
  6. Transfer lamb to a sheet tray bone side down
  7. Pour the basil crust on a flat pan or plate
  8. Bring water to boil and blanch the carrots for 2 minutes
  9. Heat olive oil in a large saute pan on medium heat, then add the cinnamon sticks, star anise, honey, and a pinch of salt
